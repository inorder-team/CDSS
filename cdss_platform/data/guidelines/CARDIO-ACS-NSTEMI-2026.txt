APPROVED CARDIOLOGY GUIDELINE: ACS / NSTEMI PATHWAY
================================================================================

SECTION 1: SCOPE AND PURPOSE
This guideline provides evidence-based recommendations for the assessment and
management of Non-ST Elevation Myocardial Infarction (NSTEMI) and Non-ST
Elevation Acute Coronary Syndrome (NSTE-ACS). It applies to adult patients
presenting with suspected ACS without ST elevation on a 12-lead ECG.

SECTION 2: NSTEMI RISK STRATIFICATION
Patients with suspected non-ST elevation acute coronary syndrome should receive
clinician-led risk stratification using symptoms, ECG findings, serial troponin
results, haemodynamic status, renal function, bleeding risk, allergies, and
relevant comorbidities.

High-risk features include: dynamic ECG changes (new ST depression ≥0.5mm,
transient ST elevation, new T-wave inversion), recurrent or refractory chest
pain at rest, signs of heart failure (pulmonary oedema, new or worsening
mitral regurgitation), haemodynamic instability (systolic BP <90mmHg, shock),
sustained ventricular arrhythmia, rising troponin above the 99th percentile
URL, and prior coronary revascularisation (PCI or CABG).

Intermediate-risk features: diabetes mellitus, chronic kidney disease
(eGFR <60 mL/min/1.73m²), ejection fraction <40%, early post-MI angina,
prior PCI/CABG without current haemodynamic compromise, elevated GRACE score.

GRACE Score should be calculated for all NSTEMI patients. A GRACE score >140
identifies high-risk patients who benefit from early invasive strategy
(coronary angiography within 24 hours).

SECTION 3: INITIAL MANAGEMENT – ALL NSTEMI PATIENTS
All patients should be assessed for:
- Oxygen if SpO2 <94%; avoid routine oxygen if SpO2 ≥94%
- IV access and continuous cardiac monitoring
- 12-lead ECG within 10 minutes and at 3, 6 hours (or with symptom change)
- Serial high-sensitivity troponin at 0h and 3h (or 0h and 1h with hs-cTnI)
- Full blood count, renal function, electrolytes, coagulation, lipid profile
- Chest X-ray (portable acceptable if haemodynamically unstable)
- Echocardiography (ECHO) should be performed in all NSTEMI patients

SECTION 4: ANTIPLATELET THERAPY – SAFETY CRITICAL BOUNDARY
Dual antiplatelet therapy (DAPT) is the cornerstone of NSTEMI management
to prevent stent thrombosis and recurrent coronary events.

Standard DAPT regimen: Aspirin 300mg loading dose followed by 75mg daily
maintenance, PLUS a P2Y12 inhibitor.

ASPIRIN ALLERGY PROTOCOL (CRITICAL SAFETY BOUNDARY):
If aspirin allergy is documented, the following protocol applies:
1. Aspirin must NOT be administered or recommended as a direct action
2. Desensitisation may be considered in selected patients by a specialist
   allergist in conjunction with the cardiology team
3. Clopidogrel monotherapy (300–600mg loading, 75mg maintenance) may be used
   as an alternative antiplatelet agent
4. Ticagrelor 180mg loading then 90mg twice daily as P2Y12 inhibitor
   (if no contraindication to ticagrelor)
5. Any deviation from standard DAPT requires cardiologist validation and
   a documented alternative plan in the clinical record
6. The recommendation must not state aspirin as a direct action without
   cardiologist validation and a documented alternative plan

P2Y12 Inhibitor Selection:
- Ticagrelor 180mg loading dose, then 90mg twice daily (preferred for NSTEMI
  managed conservatively or with PCI if not on anticoagulation)
- Prasugrel 60mg loading dose, then 10mg daily (for NSTEMI undergoing PCI;
  avoid in patients ≥75 years, weight <60kg, or prior stroke/TIA)
- Clopidogrel 300–600mg loading dose, then 75mg daily (when ticagrelor and
  prasugrel are contraindicated or not available)

Renal Impairment Considerations (CKD):
- For patients with eGFR 30–60 mL/min/1.73m²: dose adjustment may be required
  for some antithrombotics; ticagrelor is generally safe
- For patients with eGFR <30 mL/min/1.73m²: avoid prasugrel; use ticagrelor
  or clopidogrel; seek nephrology review
- Potassium monitoring is essential with ACE inhibitors/ARBs in CKD

SECTION 5: ANTICOAGULATION
All NSTEMI patients should receive anticoagulation unless contraindicated.
Options include:
- Fondaparinux 2.5mg subcutaneous once daily (preferred; lower bleeding risk)
- Enoxaparin 1mg/kg subcutaneous twice daily (dose-reduce to once daily if
  eGFR <30 or use unfractionated heparin with monitoring)
- Unfractionated heparin (UFH) IV infusion (preferred pre-PCI if immediate
  intervention planned)
- Bivalirudin (alternative to UFH + GPI during PCI)

SECTION 6: INVASIVE STRATEGY (CORONARY ANGIOGRAPHY)
High-risk NSTEMI (GRACE >140 or high-risk features): Coronary angiography
within 24 hours of presentation.

Intermediate-risk NSTEMI (GRACE 109–140): Coronary angiography within 72 hours.

Low-risk NSTEMI: Consider non-invasive ischaemia testing first; angiography
if positive or symptom-driven.

Revascularisation strategy (PCI vs CABG): Determined by coronary anatomy,
SYNTAX score, left ventricular function, comorbidities, and Heart Team
discussion. Prior PCI is relevant to the complexity of the anatomy.

SECTION 7: ADJUNCT PHARMACOTHERAPY
Beta-blockers:
- Oral beta-blockers should be initiated within 24 hours of NSTEMI in
  haemodynamically stable patients (target heart rate 50–70 bpm)
- Avoid if: acute decompensated heart failure, significant bradycardia (<50),
  heart block >1st degree, or systolic BP <100 mmHg

Statins (High-intensity):
- High-intensity statin therapy should be initiated regardless of baseline LDL
- Atorvastatin 40–80mg or Rosuvastatin 20–40mg daily
- If already on statin, maximise dose

ACE Inhibitors / ARBs:
- Initiate ACE inhibitor (or ARB if intolerant) in all NSTEMI patients with
  LVEF <40%, diabetes, anterior MI, or hypertension
- Monitor renal function and potassium closely, especially in CKD

Nitrates:
- Sublingual or IV nitrates for ongoing ischaemic pain
- Avoid if systolic BP <90 mmHg, right ventricular infarction suspected,
  or phosphodiesterase inhibitor used within 24–48 hours

SECTION 8: DIABETES MANAGEMENT IN NSTEMI
Hyperglycaemia is common in ACS and is associated with worse outcomes.
Target blood glucose: 7.8–10 mmol/L during acute phase.
SGLT2 inhibitors should be reviewed (may need to be held peri-procedure
due to risk of euglycaemic DKA with contrast use). Resume once stable.
Metformin should be withheld if eGFR <45 or during/after contrast use
until renal function is confirmed stable.

SECTION 9: MONITORING REQUIREMENTS
- Continuous ECG monitoring for minimum 24 hours in NSTEMI
- Serial troponins as per pathway
- Renal function daily in hospitalised NSTEMI patients on anticoagulation
- Potassium monitoring: target K+ 4.0–5.0 mmol/L (K+ 4.8 is borderline high;
  review ACE inhibitor/ARB dosing; avoid additional K+ supplementation)
- Blood glucose: 4–6 hourly in diabetes patients

SECTION 10: HUMAN REVIEW REQUIREMENT (AI SAFETY)
All patient-specific ACS recommendations generated by AI decision support
require cardiologist review before they are entered into the clinical record
or used for care action. The AI system must present a pending_review status
and clearly communicate that output is not to be acted upon until reviewed
and approved by a registered cardiologist or senior clinician.

SECTION 11: DISCHARGE AND SECONDARY PREVENTION
DAPT duration post-NSTEMI:
- PCI with drug-eluting stent: Minimum 6–12 months DAPT
- Medically managed: 12 months DAPT (or as guided by bleeding risk score)
- Aspirin indefinitely (or alternative if allergy)

Discharge medications checklist:
☐ Antiplatelet therapy (DAPT or monotherapy per allergy status)
☐ High-intensity statin
☐ Beta-blocker (if no contraindication)
☐ ACE inhibitor / ARB (if indicated)
☐ Aldosterone antagonist (if LVEF <40% and no significant renal impairment)

Cardiac rehabilitation: Refer all NSTEMI patients to supervised cardiac
rehabilitation programme prior to or at discharge.
