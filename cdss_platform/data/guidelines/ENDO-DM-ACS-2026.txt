GUIDELINE: TYPE 2 DIABETES MELLITUS IN ACUTE CORONARY SYNDROME
================================================================================

SECTION 1: SCOPE
This guideline applies to adult patients with pre-existing or newly diagnosed
type 2 diabetes mellitus (T2DM) presenting with acute coronary syndrome (ACS),
including NSTEMI. Diabetes is an independent risk factor for adverse
cardiovascular outcomes and increases in-hospital mortality in ACS.

SECTION 2: GLYCAEMIC MANAGEMENT DURING ACUTE ACS
Target blood glucose during acute ACS: 7.8–10.0 mmol/L (140–180 mg/dL).

Avoid hypoglycaemia: Blood glucose <4.0 mmol/L is associated with increased
mortality in ACS. Tight glycaemic control (4–6 mmol/L) is harmful acutely.

Avoid hyperglycaemia: Sustained blood glucose >14 mmol/L worsens outcomes
and increases infection risk.

Blood glucose monitoring: Every 4–6 hours during acute admission.
If on insulin infusion: hourly monitoring.

SECTION 3: ORAL ANTIDIABETIC AGENTS IN ACUTE ACS

Metformin:
- WITHHOLD during acute ACS hospitalisation if:
  a) eGFR <45 mL/min/1.73m² (risk of lactic acidosis)
  b) Haemodynamic instability
  c) Planned contrast administration (withhold 48 hours post-contrast)
  d) Significant hepatic impairment
- Resume when patient is stable, eating, eGFR ≥45, and ≥48h post-contrast

SGLT2 Inhibitors (Empagliflozin, Dapagliflozin, Canagliflozin):
- Withhold during acute ACS hospitalisation due to:
  a) Risk of euglycaemic diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA) with stress/reduced intake
  b) Risk of volume depletion compounding haemodynamic instability
  c) Peri-procedural risk of DKA with contrast use
- Resume after clinical stability, normal diet, renal function confirmed
- Note: SGLT2 inhibitors have established cardiovascular and renal benefit
  in T2DM – important to restart post-discharge

Sulphonylureas (Glipizide, Gliclazide):
- Withhold during acute ACS (high hypoglycaemia risk with variable intake)
- Restart only when eating regularly

DPP-4 Inhibitors (Sitagliptin, Saxagliptin):
- Generally safe to continue acutely; low hypoglycaemia risk
- Dose-adjust saxagliptin and sitagliptin for renal impairment
- Avoid saxagliptin in heart failure (FDA black box warning)

GLP-1 Receptor Agonists (Liraglutide, Semaglutide):
- Generally safe; low hypoglycaemia risk
- May temporarily reduce food intake (nausea); monitor for weight loss
- Established cardiovascular benefit: restart post-discharge

SECTION 4: INSULIN MANAGEMENT IN ACUTE ACS
Variable rate insulin infusion (VRII) indications:
- Blood glucose consistently >14 mmol/L
- Nil-by-mouth patients
- Patients on high-dose steroids
- Ketosis or DKA (refer to DKA pathway)

Basal-bolus insulin subcutaneous:
- For patients eating and blood glucose suboptimally controlled
- Use 50% of total daily dose as basal (e.g. glargine once daily)
- Use rapid-acting insulin with meals proportional to carbohydrate

SECTION 5: LONG-TERM CARDIOVASCULAR BENEFIT – POST-ACS DIABETES MANAGEMENT
Post-discharge diabetes management should prioritise agents with proven
cardiovascular benefit:

SGLT2 Inhibitors: Reduce hospitalisation for heart failure, renal progression,
and in some trials reduce MACE. Resume as soon as clinically appropriate.

GLP-1 Receptor Agonists (liraglutide, semaglutide): Reduce MACE in T2DM
with established cardiovascular disease. Consider initiating or continuing.

Cardiovascular targets post-ACS with T2DM:
- HbA1c: 53–58 mmol/mol (7.0–7.5%) for most patients
- For frail/elderly or high hypoglycaemia risk: HbA1c up to 69 mmol/mol (8.5%)
- Blood pressure: <130/80 mmHg
- LDL cholesterol: <1.4 mmol/L with high-intensity statin

SECTION 6: PATIENT-SPECIFIC NOTES (eGFR 42, T2DM, NSTEMI)
For a patient with eGFR 42, T2DM, and NSTEMI:
- Metformin: WITHHOLD (eGFR 42 < 45 threshold)
- SGLT2 inhibitor: WITHHOLD acutely; restart post-discharge if eGFR improves
  (check product label for eGFR threshold – typically ≥30 or ≥45 depending on agent)
- Insulin: Use if blood glucose >14 mmol/L or patient nil-by-mouth
- Monitor blood glucose 4-hourly minimum
- HbA1c at admission (if not done recently)
- Refer to diabetes team for inpatient review
