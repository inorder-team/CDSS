GUIDELINE: CHRONIC KIDNEY DISEASE (CKD) IN ACUTE CORONARY SYNDROME MANAGEMENT
================================================================================

SECTION 1: INTRODUCTION
Chronic kidney disease (CKD) is present in approximately 30–40% of patients
with ACS and is an independent predictor of short and long-term mortality.
Patients with CKD and ACS present unique challenges: increased ischaemic risk,
higher bleeding risk, contraindications to renally-cleared agents, and
contrast-associated nephropathy risk during invasive procedures.

SECTION 2: CKD STAGING RELEVANT TO ACS MANAGEMENT
Stage 3a: eGFR 45–59 mL/min/1.73m² – mild-moderate reduction
Stage 3b: eGFR 30–44 mL/min/1.73m² – moderate-severe reduction
Stage 4:  eGFR 15–29 mL/min/1.73m² – severe reduction
Stage 5:  eGFR <15 mL/min/1.73m²   – kidney failure

Patient with eGFR 42 mL/min/1.73m² is Stage 3b CKD: moderate-to-severe.

SECTION 3: ANTIPLATELET THERAPY IN CKD
CKD does not contraindicate dual antiplatelet therapy (DAPT) in NSTEMI.

Aspirin:
If aspirin allergy documented, do not use aspirin. Refer to antiplatelet
allergy protocol as per CARDIO-ACS-NSTEMI-2026.

P2Y12 Inhibitors in CKD:
- Ticagrelor: Generally safe across CKD stages including eGFR 30–60.
  No significant renal clearance of active metabolite.
  Preferred in eGFR 30–60 for predictable antiplatelet effect.
- Clopidogrel: Safe in CKD. Active metabolite not renally cleared.
  May have reduced efficacy due to CYP2C19 variability.
  Suitable alternative especially in aspirin allergy.
- Prasugrel: CONTRAINDICATED in eGFR <30. Use with caution eGFR 30–60.
  Avoid in elderly (≥75 years), low weight (<60kg), or prior stroke/TIA.

For this patient profile (eGFR 42, aspirin allergy, prior PCI, NSTEMI):
Ticagrelor 180mg loading dose then 90mg twice daily is the preferred
P2Y12 inhibitor. Clopidogrel is an acceptable alternative.
Prasugrel should be avoided given borderline renal function.

SECTION 4: ANTICOAGULATION IN CKD
Fondaparinux 2.5mg subcutaneous daily:
- Safe in eGFR 30–50 with careful monitoring
- Contraindicated if eGFR <20
- No significant dose adjustment required for eGFR 30–50

Enoxaparin in CKD:
- eGFR 30–60: Standard dosing 1mg/kg twice daily; monitor anti-Xa levels
- eGFR <30: Reduce to 1mg/kg once daily or use unfractionated heparin (UFH)
- eGFR <15: Use UFH only (fully monitored with aPTT)

Unfractionated Heparin (UFH):
- Can be used across all CKD stages
- Requires aPTT monitoring
- Preferred pre-PCI in severe CKD (eGFR <30)

SECTION 5: CONTRAST-ASSOCIATED ACUTE KIDNEY INJURY (CA-AKI)
Risk is increased in CKD (eGFR <60) undergoing coronary angiography.
Prevention strategies:
- Hydration: IV isotonic saline 1 mL/kg/h for 12 hours pre and post procedure
- Minimise contrast volume: use iso-osmolar or low-osmolar contrast
- Withhold nephrotoxic agents: NSAIDs, aminoglycosides, ACE inhibitors/ARBs
  may be withheld 24–48 hours peri-procedure (restore when haemodynamically stable)
- Withhold metformin 48 hours post-contrast if eGFR <45

SECTION 6: RENAL FUNCTION MONITORING IN ACS WITH CKD
- Serum creatinine and eGFR: Daily during admission
- Electrolytes (K+, Na+, bicarbonate): Daily; more frequently if on ACE-I/ARB
- Potassium management: Target K+ 3.5–5.0 mmol/L
  For K+ 4.8 mmol/L (borderline high): avoid additional K+ supplementation,
  withhold K+ sparing agents if eGFR <45, review ACE-I/ARB dose
- Urine output: Monitor for oliguria (sign of AKI)
- Consider nephrology review if eGFR <30 or deteriorating

SECTION 7: ACE INHIBITORS AND ARBs IN CKD-ACS
Benefits: Reduce cardiovascular mortality and slow CKD progression.
Indications in NSTEMI: LVEF <40%, diabetes, anterior MI, hypertension.

Safe initiation in CKD:
- Start at low dose; uptitrate gradually
- Monitor eGFR and K+ at 1–2 weeks after initiation or dose change
- Accept up to 20–30% rise in creatinine if K+ remains <5.5 mmol/L
- Withhold if K+ >5.5 mmol/L or eGFR falls >30% from baseline
- For this patient (eGFR 42, K+ 4.8): initiate ACE-I at low dose
  with close renal monitoring; avoid if K+ rises above 5.0 mmol/L

SECTION 8: STATIN THERAPY IN CKD
High-intensity statins are recommended in ACS regardless of CKD stage.
Atorvastatin 40–80mg: No dose adjustment required in CKD.
Rosuvastatin 20–40mg: Use with caution in severe CKD (eGFR <30).
No renal dose adjustment for atorvastatin; preferred statin in CKD.
If patient already on atorvastatin: maximise to 80mg daily for NSTEMI.

SECTION 9: DIABETES MANAGEMENT IN CKD-ACS
Metformin:
- Withhold if eGFR <45 mL/min/1.73m² (risk of lactic acidosis)
- For eGFR 42: metformin should be withheld acutely
- Restart only when renal function stable and eGFR ≥45

SGLT2 Inhibitors:
- eGFR 42: Some SGLT2 inhibitors approved down to eGFR 30–45 (check product label)
- Hold peri-procedure and post-contrast for 48 hours (euglycaemic DKA risk)
- Resume when haemodynamically stable and eating normally

Blood glucose targets during ACS: 7.8–10 mmol/L
Hypoglycaemia increases cardiovascular mortality: avoid tight control acutely.

SECTION 10: SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS FOR THIS PATIENT PROFILE
For a patient aged 68, male, NSTEMI, eGFR 42, aspirin allergy, K+ 4.8,
prior PCI, type-2 diabetes:

Priority safety points:
1. No aspirin (documented allergy) – use ticagrelor as P2Y12 inhibitor
2. Avoid prasugrel (borderline renal function, prior PCI anatomy unknown)
3. Fondaparinux preferred anticoagulant (eGFR 42 permits use; monitor)
4. Monitor K+ closely – borderline high 4.8; avoid additional K+ load
5. Withhold metformin acutely (eGFR 42 <45 threshold)
6. Hold SGLT2 inhibitor peri-procedure if applicable
7. Daily renal function monitoring
8. Hydration protocol if proceeding to coronary angiography
9. Nephrology review recommended given CKD Stage 3b in acute setting
